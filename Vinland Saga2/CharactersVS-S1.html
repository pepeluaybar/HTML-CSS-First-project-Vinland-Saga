Characters of Season 1

The warriors, the kings, the slaves of destiny.

Thorfinn Karlsefni

A child shaped by tragedy, a warrior forged in hate. Thorfinn is the embodiment of lost innocence, carrying daggers that once belonged to a man who rejected violence: his father, Thors.

His journey through Season 1 is a descent into brutality — but beneath every act of violence lies the echo of his father's words: “You have no enemies.”

His rivalry with Askeladd turns into a tragic bond. He fights not for glory, but to fill the void left by his father’s death.

See Thorfinn’s Battles

Askeladd

A puppet master, a genius tactician, a man split between two worlds. Askeladd is both villain and savior— the murderer of Thors, yet the greatest teacher Thorfinn ever had.

His philosophy becomes the backbone of Season 1: “Everyone is a slave to something.”

He manipulates kings, armies, and even fate itself. His final act — murdering the king — fractures Thorfinn’s world forever.

Explore Askeladd’s Ideals

Thors

The strongest warrior of his time — a man who chose peace over violence at the height of his power.

His legacy shapes the entire season, especially Thorfinn’s inner conflict. Thors is the moral foundation of the series, the voice that echoes long after his death.

Through him we learn the thesis of the story: “A true warrior needs no sword.”

Prince Canute

A timid boy who becomes a messianic ruler. His transformation is one of the most dramatic in the series.

Guided by Ragnar’s love and scarred by his death, Canute rises through grief to proclaim: “I shall build a paradise on Earth.”

His bond with Askeladd is both political and spiritual — and his ascension defines the climax of Season 1.
